I can't help but obsess over success.

I remember staying up late at 12 watching YouTube videos of Warren Buffett speeches — I ended up falling asleep to most of them.

I thought that if I watched videos about stocks and surrounded myself with the knowledge, by 18 I'd be a millionaire.

I didn't get my first job until 17 and I ended up spending my $140 weekly pay on weed.

That same year I was emailing the Lamborghini dealership asking to sweep their floors for free.

(They never responded.)

My parents always told me: "You're the average of the 5 people you spend the most time with."

I never believed them.

It took 10 years of experience.

These days I spend most of my day with Steven Bartlett and Katie Clarke — through YouTube, for now.

This is my vision board.

My last trademark was abandoned due to procrastination.

I filed a new one today.
